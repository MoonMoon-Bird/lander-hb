Implied &Historical Volatility

Instructed by Options Industry Council

With any options strategy, there are two types of volatility to consider historical and implied. Historical volatility is a measure of how much underlying movement has already transpired, while implied volatility, or IV, is an indication of how much change the market is expecting based on the optionsprice.

During this free presentation from OICSM, attendees will learnabout:
